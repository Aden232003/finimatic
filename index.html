Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the Investment Authority Amplification System

IAAS System

What exactly is the Investment Authority Amplification System (IAAS)?

IAAS is our comprehensive systematic implementation that transforms your existing expertise into market authority. It includes 5 integrated services: SignalStack™ (market intelligence), ContentOps™ (content creation), Relatrix™ (relationship building), LeadLogic™ (lead generation), and ToolFoundry™ (custom microSaaS-tools). We take your research, presentations, and expertise and amplify them across multiple platforms to attract qualified prospects.

Can I access individual parts of the IAAS instead of the full system?

Yes, you can access individual services. Only ToolFoundry™ can be accessed completely independently. The other services (ContentOps™, Relatrix™, LeadLogic™) can also be accessed individually, but they require SignalStack™ alongside them. This is because we need the complete research and market intelligence from SignalStack™ to create effective content, personalization angles for outreach, and strategic positioning frameworks. SignalStack™ provides the foundation that makes all other services effective.

Are there discounts for purchasing the entire IAAS together?

Yes, there are significant discounts when you invest in the complete IAAS system rather than individual services. Contact us to discuss a personalized package that fits your specific needs and budget.

Process & Timeline

How long does it take to see results?

Initial content creation begins after 2-3 weeks of SignalStack™ delivery, as the first few weeks focus on comprehensive market intelligence and research. Qualified prospect engagement typically starts within 45-60 days, with measurable authority building evident by month 3 completion.

Can we see the content before it goes live?

Absolutely. You review and approve all content before publication. We work collaboratively to ensure everything aligns with your brand voice and expertise positioning.
